# Landing the Design-Partner Dealer (GTM track)

Goal: sign **one** mid-size contract-furniture dealer as a design partner who gives us
(a) their catalog + price book (SIF/pCon export), (b) their discount bands, and (c) 2–3
historical projects (plate + final BOM + quote) to benchmark Gates A–C. In return they get
early access and shape the product. This runs in parallel with the Phase 0/1 build.

---

## 1. Who to target (and who to avoid)

**Ideal:** an independent contract-furniture dealer, **~10–50 people**, that does its own
pre-sales test-fits with **one or two overworked CET/2020 designers**. They feel the pain of
slow pre-sales budgeting directly, and a principal can say yes in one meeting.

**Avoid (for the pilot):**
- **Mega-dealers** (100+ staff, multiple branches): big in-house design teams, slow procurement,
  committee decisions. They don't feel the pain and won't move fast.
- **Pure e-com furniture sellers / office liquidators:** no test-fit workflow, wrong ICP.
- **Brand-owned showrooms** locked to one manufacturer's tooling.

**Qualification checklist (a dealer is a fit if ≥4 are true):**
- [ ] Sells contract/commercial furniture (Steelcase / MillerKnoll / Haworth / Knoll / etc.), not just resi.
- [ ] 10–50 employees, single metro or 1–2 locations.
- [ ] Has at least one designer running **CET Designer** or **2020 Worksheet** (they can export SIF).
- [ ] Does **pre-sales test-fits / budgets** to win projects (not just fulfilling specified orders).
- [ ] Principal/owner or VP Sales is reachable (not a faceless corporate).
- [ ] Active recent project flow (LinkedIn posts, "we just completed…", hiring designers).

## 2. Where to find named dealers (per metro)

1. **Manufacturer "find a dealer" locators** — the highest-signal source (confirms they're
   authorized + which lines they carry):
   - Steelcase dealer locator, MillerKnoll/Herman Miller "find a retailer/dealer", Haworth
     "find a dealer", Knoll dealer locator. Enter the target ZIP, list every authorized dealer.
2. **MMQB / industry press** (Monday Morning Quarterback, officeinsight, Work Design Magazine) —
   names dealers, M&A, who's growing.
3. **LinkedIn** — search `contract furniture dealer <metro>`; filter company size 11–50. Find the
   principal / VP Sales / lead designer. Check for posts about test-fits or new projects.
4. **IIDA / local AIA chapters & CRE brokers** — brokers know every furniture dealer in town and
   can warm-intro you.
5. **BIFMA member list** and dealer-association directories.

> Tell me the **target metro** and I'll pull a named shortlist (dealer, size estimate, lines
> carried, the person to contact) from the manufacturer locators + LinkedIn.

## 3. The pitch (frame: design partner, not a sale)

You are **not** selling software yet. You want a validation partner. The ask is small and
flattering, and it gets you exactly the data the build needs.

**One-line value prop:**
> "Turn a floor plate into a test-fit **and** a budgetary quote in minutes — so you chase more
> deals and only spend your CET designer's time on the ones that convert."

**Why they say yes:** pre-sales test-fits + budgets are slow, unpaid, and often for prospects who
ghost. Anything that compresses that to minutes lets them pursue more opportunities with the same
team. You're selling **speed in pre-sales**, not replacing CET.

## 5. The discovery call (20–30 min)

**Listen first — confirm the pain:**
- Walk me through how you produce a test-fit + budget for a new prospect today.
- How long does it take? Who does it? How many do you do that never convert?
- What tools — CET, 2020, pCon? Who maintains the catalog/pricing?

**Then show + ask:**
- Show the Phase 0 quote running on synthetic data; explain the test-fit is next.
- The data ask (Section 6).

**Close:** "Can I validate this on 2–3 of your past projects this month? You'll see whether my
budgetary number lands within ~10–15% of what you actually quoted."

---

## 6. The data ask (what we need from them)

| Need | Why | Form |
|---|---|---|
| **Catalog + price book** | Populate the catalog with their real SKUs + list prices | SIF or pCon export of their library / a representative project |
| **Discount bands** | List-minus by manufacturer for budgetary net | A simple list (e.g. "Steelcase 48%, MillerKnoll 50%…") |
| **Install / freight / tax assumptions** | Complete the budgetary total | Their rule-of-thumb % or a sample invoice |
| **2–3 historical projects** | Benchmark Gates A (quote) & C (test-fit) | For each: floor plate (DXF/PDF), final BOM, the quoted total |

Frame it as: "Whatever you can export from CET or pCon works — I built the ingest around SIF
specifically so you don't have to do anything manual."
